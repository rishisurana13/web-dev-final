Introduction

The essential story being told by this website is the celebration of footballing legends, particularly Cristiano Ronaldo, Lionel Messi, and Neymar (with the promise of adding more daily). The website provides fans with access to information about these players, including their biographies, career statistics, and interesting facts. The site aims to be a go-to source for fans who want to stay up to date with their favorite players and learn more about their journey to becoming legends of the game.

The website has a clear and straightforward structure, with a navigation bar at the top of the page that allows users to easily access information about each of the featured players. The homepage provides a brief introduction to the website's purpose and what visitors can expect to find. The main content is divided into sections, including the header, home banner, and sections containing welcome text. The footer section contains a concluding message to thank visitors for their interest in the website.

Inspiration

  1. Websites of football clubs and associations, which showcase player bios, stats, and other details
  2. Football magazines and blogs, which offer analysis, insights, and opinions about various players and teams
  3. Social media platforms, where fans interact and share their thoughts about footballers and matches

Accessibility

  1. The website is designed to be responsive and can be accessed on different devices such as desktops, laptops, tablets, and smartphones. The use of the meta viewport tag and responsive design techniques such as using relative units for font sizes and media queries to adjust layout for different screen sizes makes the site accessible on a wide range of devices.
  2. The website's navigation menu is easy to use and clearly identifies the different pages, making it easy for users to find the content they are looking for.
  3. The website follows accessibility standards, such as using descriptive alt text for images and ensuring that the site is navigable using keyboard-only commands for users who may have disabilities that prevent them from using a mouse. The use of semantic HTML tags such as nav, header, main and section makes the site more accessible to users who rely on assistive technologies such as screen readers.
  4. The use of the "title" attribute in the navigation links provides additional information about the content of the linked pages to users who may have difficulty interpreting the context of the link based solely on its text.

Usability

  1. Navigation: The site's navigation is designed to be intuitive and easy to use. The navigation bar is located at the top of the page, making it easy for users to find the information they're looking for. Each link is clearly labeled, and the active page is highlighted, providing users with clear feedback on their location within the site.
  2. Readability: The site's content is designed to be easy to read and understand. The font size, typeface, and color scheme are all carefully selected to ensure that the text is legible and easy on the eyes. Headings and subheadings are used to break up long blocks of text, making it easier for users to scan and find the information they need.
  3. Responsiveness: The site is designed to be responsive and mobile-friendly, meaning that it adapts to different screen sizes and devices. This ensures that users can access the site and its content from any device, including smartphones and tablets. The site's layout is also designed to be flexible, so that it can accommodate different screen sizes without compromising usability.
